Fiscal representation for VAT in France

We act as your French fiscal representative for VAT, handling registrations, returns and communication with the tax office so you can operate in France with confidence.

What is French fiscal representation?

When a non-French business has VAT obligations in France, it often needs to appoint a fiscal representative to act on its behalf with the French tax authorities. This representative takes on certain responsibilities, including ensuring VAT returns are filed correctly and on time, and serving as the official point of contact for the tax office.

Fiscal representation is particularly relevant for businesses established outside the European Union that are required to register for French VAT. The representative helps bridge the gap between the foreign business and the French tax administration, ensuring compliance with local requirements.

At VAT EPR EXPERT FRANCE, we serve as your fiscal representative, handling all interactions with the tax authorities so you can focus on your business while remaining fully compliant with French VAT rules.

Who this service is for

Our VAT representation service is designed for businesses that need to comply with French VAT requirements but are not established in France.

Non-French businesses selling to French customers

Companies based outside France that sell goods or services to French consumers and have triggered French VAT obligations.

Non-EU sellers needing French VAT registration

Businesses established outside the EU that are required to register for VAT in France and need a fiscal representative.

Sellers using fulfilment centres or marketplaces

E-commerce sellers storing goods in France or selling via marketplaces where French VAT registration is required.

Partners managing multiple seller clients

Tax firms, logistics providers and platforms looking to centralise French VAT compliance for their seller clients.

Common questions about French VAT representation

Do all non-French businesses need a fiscal representative?

Not necessarily. EU-based businesses typically do not need a fiscal representative, though they may still need to register for French VAT. Non-EU businesses with French VAT obligations generally do need to appoint a fiscal representative.

What is the difference between a fiscal representative and a tax agent?

A fiscal representative assumes joint liability for VAT obligations, while a tax agent simply acts on behalf of the business without assuming liability. The choice depends on the business's situation and origin.

How long does VAT registration take?

The timeline varies depending on the completeness of documentation and the tax office workload, but typically ranges from a few weeks to a couple of months. We work to ensure applications are complete and follow up proactively.
