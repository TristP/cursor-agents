Frequently asked questions

Find answers to common questions about French VAT representation, EPR compliance, and how we work with partners and sellers.

French VAT

You may need to register for French VAT if you store goods in France, sell to French consumers above certain thresholds, or have other activities that create a French VAT obligation. The specific requirements depend on your business model, location, and sales patterns. We can help assess your situation during an initial consultation.

A fiscal representative assumes joint liability with the business for VAT obligations. A tax agent acts on behalf of the business but does not assume this liability. Non-EU businesses registering for VAT in France typically need a fiscal representative, while EU businesses may use a tax agent.

Registration timelines vary depending on the completeness of documentation and current processing times at the tax office. It typically takes between a few weeks and a couple of months. We ensure applications are complete and follow up proactively to minimise delays.

Most businesses file monthly VAT returns (CA3) in France. However, smaller businesses may qualify for quarterly filing. We will advise on the appropriate filing frequency for your situation and handle all return preparation and submission on your behalf.

EU businesses are generally not required to appoint a fiscal representative in France. However, they may still benefit from working with a tax agent or compliance partner who can handle VAT registrations, returns, and communication with the tax authorities.

EPR in France

France has one of the most comprehensive EPR systems in Europe, covering categories including packaging, electronics (WEEE), batteries, textiles, furniture, tyres, paper and print materials, DIY and garden products, toys, sports equipment, and more. The most common for e-commerce sellers are packaging and, depending on the products sold, electronics and textiles.

A UIN (Unique Identification Number) is issued when you register with an eco-organisation. Marketplaces operating in France, such as Amazon, require sellers to provide valid UINs to demonstrate EPR compliance before allowing sales. Without valid UINs, your listings may be restricted or removed.

Declaration frequency varies by eco-organisation and producer size. Most categories require annual declarations, typically in the first quarter of the year for the previous year's data. Some eco-organisations may require more frequent reporting for larger producers. We handle all declaration preparation and submission on your behalf.

Non-compliance can result in financial penalties from regulatory authorities. Additionally, marketplaces may restrict or suspend your selling privileges if you cannot demonstrate valid EPR registrations. It is important to register and maintain compliance proactively to avoid disruptions to your business.

If you are not established in France but place regulated products on the French market, you typically need to appoint an authorised representative (mandataire) who is based in France. This representative acts on your behalf with the eco-organisations and regulatory authorities.
