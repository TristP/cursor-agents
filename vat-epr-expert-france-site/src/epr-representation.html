Authorised representative for EPR in France

We help you and your clients meet French Extended Producer Responsibility obligations by acting as authorised representative with the relevant eco-organisations.

What is EPR in France?

Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) is an environmental policy framework that makes producers responsible for the entire lifecycle of their products, including end-of-life management. In France, this means that businesses placing certain products on the French market must register with approved eco-organisations and contribute to the collection, recycling and proper disposal of those products.

France has one of the most comprehensive EPR systems in Europe, covering a wide range of product categories. Each category is managed by one or more approved eco-organisations, and producers must register, report quantities placed on the market, and pay eco-contributions based on their declarations.

For non-French companies, fulfilling these obligations typically requires appointing an authorised representative based in France to act on their behalf with the eco-organisations and regulatory authorities.

Who needs an authorised representative?

If you are not established in France but place regulated products on the French market, you likely need an authorised representative.

Non-French companies placing regulated products on the French market

Businesses based outside France that sell products falling under EPR categories to French consumers.

Online sellers and producers shipping packaged goods to France

E-commerce businesses sending products in packaging to French customers, triggering packaging EPR obligations.

Partners ensuring their clients are compliant

Platforms, logistics providers and agencies helping their seller clients meet French EPR requirements.

Common questions about French EPR

What is a UIN and why do I need one?

A UIN (Unique Identification Number) is issued when you register with an eco-organisation. Marketplaces operating in France often require sellers to provide valid UINs to demonstrate EPR compliance before allowing sales.

How often do I need to make declarations?

Declaration frequency varies by eco-organisation and producer size, but is typically annual for most categories. Some eco-organisations may require more frequent reporting for larger producers.

What happens if I do not comply with EPR requirements?

Non-compliance can result in penalties and may prevent you from selling on marketplaces that verify EPR compliance. Additionally, regulatory authorities may take enforcement action against non-compliant producers.
