France VAT for non-EU e-commerce sellers: where to start

An overview of French VAT obligations for non-EU sellers shipping goods to French customers, including registration requirements and key considerations.

Tristan Perot

Founder, VAT EPR EXPERT FRANCE

If you are a non-EU business selling goods to customers in France, understanding your French VAT obligations is essential. This guide provides an overview of when registration is required and what steps you should take to ensure compliance.

When do non-EU sellers need to register for French VAT?

Non-EU sellers may trigger French VAT registration requirements in several scenarios. The most common situations include:

  • Storing goods in France (for example, using a French fulfilment centre)
  • Selling goods directly to French consumers when the goods are shipped from within the EU
  • Importing goods into France for distribution
  • Meeting certain distance selling thresholds when selling across EU borders

The specific rules depend on your business model, where your goods are located, and how they are shipped to French customers. If you are unsure whether you need to register, we recommend seeking professional advice tailored to your situation.

The role of a fiscal representative

When a non-EU business registers for VAT in France, it is generally required to appoint a fiscal representative. This representative takes on joint liability for the VAT obligations and acts as the official point of contact with the French tax authorities.

A fiscal representative is different from a tax agent. The key distinction is liability: a fiscal representative shares responsibility for VAT compliance, while a tax agent simply acts on behalf of the business without assuming liability.

Choosing the right fiscal representative is important. You want a partner who understands e-commerce, can handle the specific requirements of your business, and provides clear communication throughout the process.

Key steps in the registration process

The French VAT registration process involves several key steps:

1. Gather required documentation

You will need to provide company registration documents, identification for directors, details of your business activities, and information about your expected sales in France.

2. Appoint a fiscal representative

Work with your chosen representative to complete the necessary appointment documentation and power of attorney.

3. Submit the registration application

Your representative will submit the application to the appropriate tax office and follow up on progress.

4. Receive your VAT number

Once approved, you will receive your French VAT number and can begin fulfilling your obligations.

Ongoing compliance

Registration is just the beginning. Once registered, you will need to:

  • File periodic VAT returns (typically monthly in France)
  • Pay VAT owed to the tax authorities
  • Maintain proper records of your transactions
  • Respond to any queries from the tax office

Working with an experienced partner can help ensure these obligations are met accurately and on time.
